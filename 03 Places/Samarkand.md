# Samarkand

The non-negotiable Silk Road stop. Tamerlane's capital, late 14th–early 15th century — turquoise iwans, monumental Timurid scale. UNESCO listed. We arrive Day 9 (trek out + border), spend Days 10–11.

`role:: cultural anchor`
`days_here:: 2.5`

## The hit list

### Shah-i-Zinda — at sunrise
**The move.** Alley of tile mausoleums from the 11th–15th centuries. Empty at first light, golden side-light hits the cobalt and turquoise tile work head-on.
- Opens 07:00 (verify in October — may shift to 08:00 as days shorten)
- Plan 90 min minimum
- Entry: [shah_i_zinda_uzs:: 50000] (~$4)
- **Day 10 priority — set alarms.** After 09:30 it's tour groups end-to-end.

### Registan — at sunset + blue hour
Three madrassas around a plaza. Iconic, monumental. Visit twice if possible:
- **Daytime visit** — go inside the madrassas (each is a small bazaar of artisan shops, mostly tourist tat but a few decent miniatures)
- **Sunset + blue hour** — sit across the plaza in the small park. The lights come on, blue hour deepens, no admission needed for the external view.
- Entry to madrassa courtyards: [registan_uzs:: 65000] (~$5.5)

### Bibi-Khanym Mosque
Tamerlane's wife's mosque, vast scale, partly ruin, partly reconstruction. Goes well with Siyob Bazaar lunch since they're adjacent.

### Gur-e-Amir
Tamerlane's tomb. Smaller scale than Registan but the most refined Timurid interior — the gold-leaf vaulting is incredible. ~45 min.

### Ulugh Beg Observatory
North edge of town, ~15-min taxi. 15th-century astronomical observatory — the 40m-diameter sextant is preserved in a trench, the small museum has good context on Ulugh Beg (Tamerlane's grandson, astronomer-king). Worth 1 hour.

### Afrosiyob Museum
On site of pre-Mongol Samarkand. Excavations of Sogdian/Persian-era city + 7th-century **Hall of Ambassadors** frescoes (Sogdian, Korean, Chinese envoys depicted — Silk Road in pictures). Often skipped, shouldn't be. ~1 hour.

### Siyob Bazaar
Adjacent to Bibi-Khanym, working bazaar — dried fruit, spices, Samarkand non bread (largest in UZ, stamped pattern), nuts. Lunch from street stalls (samsa, shashlik).

### Shahrisabz (day trip, optional)
- Tamerlane's birthplace, 80 km south, ~1.5h drive
- Ruins of Ak-Saray Palace, Dorut Tilavat complex
- Full-day commitment — skip unless adding Day 11.5 buffer

## Things to skip

- **"Eternal City" (Boqiy Shahar) theme-park complex** — built 2022, polished tourist trap, fake Silk Road street. Skip unless desperate or with kids.
- Most carpet/tour shops in Registan madrassas (unless we want overpriced silk for gifts)

## Where to eat

### Lunch (plov peak, ~12:00–14:00)
- **Karimbek** — long-running plov spot, locals + tourists, near Siyob Bazaar area
- Samarkand-style plov: meat + rice **layered, presented unmixed** (vs. Tashkent's heavier mixed version). Lighter, more carrots, fewer raisins.
- Street samsa from tandoor ovens at Siyob Bazaar — 5–10k UZS each

### Dinner
- **Platan** — central, garden setting, reliable Russian-Uzbek mix, English menu, mid-range
- **Marokand** — modern Uzbek, more design-focused, near Registan
- Hotel restaurants generally good in Samarkand — high-end traveler market means quality

### Wine?
Samarkand has nascent wineries — **Samarkand Wine Center** does tastings. Uzbek wine is improving (post-Soviet revival), lean dry whites are best bets. Worth a stop if curious.

## Where to sleep

Old town near Registan is the move — walk everywhere.

- **Bibi-Khanym Hotel** — boutique, traditional courtyard, walking distance to everything, ~$80–120 twin
- **Hotel Malika Prime** — mid-range, reliable, central, ~$50–70 twin
- **Antica B&B** — guesthouse, family-run, character, ~$30–50 twin
- Old-town homestays via Booking — cheaper, varying quality

## Logistics

### Arrival from Penjikent (Day 9)
- Shared taxi Penjikent → border → Samarkand, ~$20 per person, 2.5–3h
- Border at Sarazm/Jartepa, 24/7, see [[Visas]]

### To Tashkent (Day 12)
- **Afrosiyob** high-speed train, 2h 10m, ~$25 economy, ~$45 business
- Book 1–2 weeks ahead at [railway.uz](https://railway.uz/) — sells out
- Departs Samarkand station mid-morning typically (8:00 and 17:00 most days)
- Station is 10 min taxi from old town

### Getting around
- Old town is walkable end-to-end (Registan to Bibi-Khanym to Shah-i-Zinda is ~30 min walk total)
- Yandex Go works for longer hops (Ulugh Beg, Afrosiyob Museum, train station)

## Two-traveler logistics
- **Mosque dress for her** — shoulders + knees in all sites. Headcovering: scarf required at the active mosque inside Bibi-Khanym, but most "mosques" we visit are now museum-only (no covering needed). Shoes off everywhere except plazas.
- **Photo rules** — generally fine, including inside madrassas. Don't shoot during active prayer.
