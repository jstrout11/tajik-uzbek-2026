# Money

## Currencies
- **Tajikistan:** Somoni (TJS). Rate as of May 2026: ~**[usd_to_tjs:: 9.4] TJS = 1 USD**
- **Uzbekistan:** Som (UZS). Rate as of May 2026: ~**[usd_to_uzs:: 12130] UZS = 1 USD**

> [!warning] Rates fluctuate
> The UZ som has steadily depreciated; the TJS has been more stable. Check [xe.com USD/TJS](https://www.xe.com/currencyconverter/convert/?Amount=1&From=USD&To=TJS) and [xe.com USD/UZS](https://www.xe.com/currencyconverter/convert/?Amount=1&From=USD&To=UZS) the week of departure. Re-confirm before any large exchange.

## Cash strategy

### Bring USD — bills must be clean
- ~**$500–700 USD per person** in **$50s and $100s**, plus a few $20s and $10s
- **No tears, no marks, no ink, no folds at the corners** — torn or marked bills get rejected outright
- Get fresh bills from bank teller (not ATM) the week before

### Tajikistan — mostly cash culture
- Cards accepted at higher-end Dushanbe hotels and a handful of restaurants only
- **Hit ATM in Dushanbe before heading to Fann.** No ATMs in Penjikent's villages or on the trek.
- Reliable ATMs: Dushanbe, Khujand, Penjikent town center
- **Visa works** at most ATMs; **Mastercard limited** (Kazkom ATMs only, found in Dushanbe and Khujand)
- Daily cash withdrawal limits often ~2000 TJS (~$210)

### Uzbekistan — cards work in cities, cash everywhere else
- Cards (Visa/MC) accepted at most mid-range hotels, decent restaurants in Samarkand/Tashkent
- ATMs reliable in Tashkent, Samarkand
- **Bazaar moneychangers are now legal** (post-2017 reforms) and often beat bank rates — Chorsu and Siyob both have them

## How much to bring on the trek

For [[Kulikalon-Alaudin Loop]] (5 days, no ATM access):
- Horseman + horse: [horseman_per_day_tjs:: 300] TJS/day × 5 = 1500 TJS (~$160)
- Food provisioning from [[Penjikent]] bazaar: ~800 TJS for two (~$85)
- Local guesthouse (if mixing camping with homestay): ~150 TJS/night × 2 (~$32 split)
- Tips: 200–500 TJS
- **Bring ~2500–3000 TJS per pair (~$270–320)** in cash from Dushanbe ATM before departure

## Tipping
- Restaurants in cities: 5–10%, often included as "service" on bill — check
- Drivers (multi-day): round up significantly, ~10%
- Horseman + porter: ~10% of trek fee at end, on top of agreed daily rate
- Bazaar bargaining: expected; offer ~50–60% of opening price, settle ~70%

## Action items
- [ ] Order fresh $50s and $100s from bank — **2 weeks before flight**
- [ ] Notify credit card companies of travel
- [ ] Bring backup Visa debit card (separate from primary)
- [ ] Confirm card has no foreign transaction fees, or accept the loss
- [ ] First day in Dushanbe: hit ATM, withdraw ~3000 TJS, then exchange some USD at official rate at bank or hotel for backup TJS
