# Phrases

Three languages get you through. **Russian** is the regional lingua franca — anyone over 30 in either country speaks it. **Tajik** (Persian-family, written in Cyrillic in TJ) and **Uzbek** (Turkic, mostly Latin script post-2023 transition; some Cyrillic on signage) are bonus goodwill.

> [!tip] Practical priority
> 1. Russian first (30 phrases below)
> 2. **Assalomu alaykum** as universal greeting in both countries
> 3. A few Tajik phrases when in TJ, a few Uzbek phrases when in UZ

## Cyrillic crash course
Useful for reading signs in TJ (and older UZ signs).

| Letter | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| А а | A | a |
| Б б | (no Latin equiv) | b |
| В в | B | **v** (not b!) |
| Г г | r | g |
| Д д | (no Latin equiv) | d |
| Е е | E | ye |
| Ж ж | (no Latin equiv) | zh (like "s" in "measure") |
| З з | 3 | z |
| И и | (backwards N) | ee |
| К к | K | k |
| Л л | (Λ) | l |
| М м | M | m |
| Н н | H | **n** (not h!) |
| О о | O | o |
| П п | (Π) | p |
| Р р | P | **r** (not p!) |
| С с | C | **s** (not c!) |
| Т т | T | t |
| У у | Y | u |
| Ф ф | (Φ) | f |
| Х х | X | kh |
| Ш ш | (W) | sh |
| Щ щ | (similar W) | shch |
| Ы ы | (no Latin equiv) | ih (back of throat) |
| Ю ю | (10) | yu |
| Я я | (R backwards) | ya |

Tajik adds: Ҷ (j), Ҳ (h), Ғ (gh), Қ (q), Ӣ (long ee).

## Working with locals

> [!tip] In remote TJ villages
> Older people may speak **only Tajik**, no Russian. Younger people (~40 and under) speak Russian fine. Body language and a Tajik phrasebook on your phone (offline-saved Google Translate) close gaps.

> [!tip] In UZ cities
> **English is rising** in Samarkand, Bukhara, Tashkent among under-40s, especially in tourism-adjacent roles (hotels, guides). Russian still default with older generations and outside tourist areas.
