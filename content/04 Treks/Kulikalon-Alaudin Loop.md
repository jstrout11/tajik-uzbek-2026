# Kulikalon–Alaudin Loop

The standard Fann trek. Lake-and-pass classic, mostly 2500–3000m with one push to **Alaudin Pass at 3860m**. Most October-viable of the serious Fann routes — pass is below the October snow line in normal years, alpine lakes are the visual headline.

| Trek | Days | Max pass | Oct viable | Logistics |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| **[[Kulikalon-Alaudin Loop]]** | 5 | **3860m** | **Yes** | Hireable, well-trodden |

The Alaudin Pass elevation (3860m) sits below the typical first-snow line for mid-October. Routes higher than ~4000m become weather lotteries in October. Kulikalon-Alaudin gives us real Fann scenery — turquoise lakes, granite, big country — without rolling dice on a 4500m+ pass.

## Route

### Day 1 (Trek Day 1, itinerary Day 5)
**Artuch Alplager (2200m) → Chukurak Valley → Chukurak Pass (3180m) → Kulikalon Lakes (2800m)**
- Distance: ~10 km
- Elevation gain: ~1000m, loss: ~400m
- Time: 6–7h with horseman pace + lunch
- Trail: well-marked, gradual climb through juniper forest then alpine meadow, switchbacks to pass
- Camp: above Bibijonat Lake or main Kulikalon Lake at ~2800m

### Day 2 (Trek Day 2, itinerary Day 6) — **exploration day**
**Day around Kulikalon Lakes**
- Side hike to **Mutnoe Lakes** or upper basin (gain 200–400m and return)
- Photograph the lake-and-spire setup
- Easy day before Alaudin Pass tomorrow
- Camp: same site

### Day 3 (Trek Day 3, itinerary Day 7) — **the big day**
**Kulikalon Lakes (2800m) → Alaudin Pass (3860m) → Alaudin Lakes (2800m)**
- Distance: ~12 km
- Elevation gain: ~1100m, loss: ~1100m
- Time: 8–10h
- Trail: steady climb on scree to pass, **then steep descent** — trekking poles essential, microspikes if early snow
- **Start early.** Alpine starts 06:00 in pre-dawn dark, on pass by 11:00, descending by midday
- Camp: lower Alaudin Lake, ~2800m

### Day 4 (Trek Day 4, itinerary Day 8)
**Alaudin Lakes day — explore + optional Mutnye Lakes**
- Day hike option: up to Mutnye Lakes (gain ~400m, ~4h round trip)
- Or rest and enjoy lake
- Camp: same site

### Day 5 (Trek Day 5, itinerary Day 9) — **trek out + transit**
**Alaudin Lakes → trailhead road (Vertical Alauddin / Sarvoda area) → vehicle to Penjikent**
- Distance: ~8 km
- Elevation loss: ~800m
- Time: 4–5h
- Vehicle pickup pre-arranged at trailhead
- Then: drive to Penjikent (lunch break), cross Sarazm/Jartepa border, drive to Samarkand
- **Long day** — finish ~21:00 in Samarkand if all smooth

## October-specific concerns

> [!warning] Snow on Alaudin Pass
> 3860m is just below the typical mid-October snow line, but a cold snap or early storm can drop snow on the pass. **Bring microspikes + gaiters.** If overnight storm closes the pass: retreat over Chukurak Pass back to Artuch (longer but lower exit).

> [!warning] Horseman availability
> Horsemen often wind down operations by mid-October. **Pre-arrange via [Paramount Journey](https://paramountjourney.com/), [Hiking Tajikistan](https://hikingtajikistan.com/), or [ZTDA](https://ztda-tourism.tj/)** at least 4 weeks before. Walk-up at Artuch is unreliable in October.

> [!warning] Night temperatures
> Camps at 2800m drop to **–5 to –10°C** at night in October. Two-person 4-season tent, down jacket (not synthetic), sleeping pad with R-value 4+ — see [[Packing]].

> [!tip] Weather windows
> Watch [Mountain-Forecast Alaudin](https://www.mountain-forecast.com/) the week before. If a 5+ day clear window appears at the start of the trek, you're set. If forecast is a wall of storms, pivot to [[Seven Lakes (Haft Kul)]] — lower, shorter, mostly cultural.

### Independent vs. guided
**Independent + horseman:** the standard. Horseman knows route, sets camp, basic Russian common. We handle our own decisions. Best for an experienced mountaineer pair. **Cost: ~$350 total for two.**

**Full guided:** add an English-speaking guide for ~$50–80/day. Useful if there's any uncertainty about navigation in cloud / snow conditions, but on a marked route like Kulikalon-Alaudin it's overkill. **Cost: +$250–400 over 5 days for two.**

> [!tip] Our recommendation
> Independent + horseman. The route is well-trodden, the horseman covers logistics, and the mountaineer's experience covers nav/snow judgment. Save the guide budget.
