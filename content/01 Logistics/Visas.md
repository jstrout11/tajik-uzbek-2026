# Visas

## Tajikistan — e-visa required
- Portal: **[evisa.tj](https://www.evisa.tj/)** (Ministry of Foreign Affairs)
- Fee: [tj_evisa_cost_usd:: 30] single-entry, paid by credit card
- Processing time: [tj_evisa_processing_days:: 5] typical (often 1–3 working days)
- Validity: up to 45 days
- Apply window: ~6–8 weeks before travel (early–mid August 2026)

> [!warning] GBAO permit — **NOT needed** for this trip
> The GBAO permit ([gbao_cost_usd:: 20] extra during e-visa application) is only required for the **Pamir region east of Khorog**. Fann Mountains and everything on our itinerary are outside GBAO. Do **not** check the GBAO box during application — it adds processing time and we don't need it.

### Required for application
- Passport scan (6+ months validity beyond entry, 2+ blank pages)
- Digital photo (passport-style)
- Itinerary outline (rough is fine)
- Credit card

- [ ] Apply for TJ e-visa via [evisa.tj](https://www.evisa.tj/) by **2026-08-15** (allow buffer)
- [ ] Print 2 copies — one in pack, one in passport sleeve
- [ ] Photocopy passport, keep separate

## Uzbekistan — visa-free for US citizens
As of **January 1, 2026**, US citizens get **30 days visa-free** entry to Uzbekistan (presidential decree, Nov 3, 2025).

- Stay limit: [uz_visa_free_days:: 30] days
- Passport: must have 3 months' validity beyond intended departure
- No application or registration needed for stays under 30 days (hotels handle automatic registration)

> [!tip] Verify before departure
> Visa policies change. Re-confirm via [travel.state.gov Uzbekistan](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/international-travel/International-Travel-Country-Information-Pages/Uzbekistan.html) two weeks before flight.

### Action
- [ ] Re-verify UZ visa-free for US citizens (check 2 weeks before travel)
- [ ] Bring printed first-night accommodation confirmation (UZ border occasionally asks)

## Border crossings — this trip

### Sarazm/Jartepa (Penjikent → Samarkand) — Day 9
- Status: **open**, re-opened 2018 after long closure
- Hours: 24/7 (no lunch break)
- Distance: 45 km from Samarkand, 30 km from Penjikent
- Process: walk between checkpoints, taxi on both sides. Generally smooth, under 1 hour for foreigners.
- Contact (Jartepa side, 2024): +998 66-230-42-42

> [!warning] Border status check
> Sarazm/Jartepa has been reliable since 2018, but Central Asia borders can close on short notice (political tension, COVID-era patterns). Check [Caravanistan UZ-TJ borders](https://caravanistan.com/border-crossings/uzbekistan/) two weeks before departure.

### Not used this trip
- **Oybek** (Tashkent ↔ Khujand) — alternative if Sarazm closes; would mean rerouting through Khujand
- **Kyzyl-Art** (TJ → Kyrgyzstan, Osh) — Pamir-only exit
