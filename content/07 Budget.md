## Sanity checks
- Trek days are bottoming at ~$46 — almost entirely the horseman share ($32/day) plus food. Lodging on trek is essentially $0.
- Most expensive days are transit days (Day 9, Day 12) — paying for taxis/train and a new city lodging same day
- Tashkent and Samarkand are the daily-cost peaks for cities (entries, mid-range hotels), but each is ~$50/day, not $80
- Pre-trip is **light** because Colorado-based travelers already own the gear — $50 placeholder for gap items (microspikes, gaiters)
- If you booked TK V-V economy round-trip from DEN, you're likely in the $1,100–1,500 range — actuals win, fill it in

## Cash flow plan
- Start with $600 USD cash per person (down from $700 — we don't need as much)
- ATM in Dushanbe Day 1 or 2: withdraw 2500 TJS (~$265) — covers trek + Penjikent stays
- Use cards in Samarkand + Tashkent hotels + better restaurants
- Bazaar moneychangers in UZ for any USD-to-UZS conversion (better rates than banks)
