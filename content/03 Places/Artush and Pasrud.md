# Artush and Pasrud (trailheads)

Two valley villages serving as the launch points for Fann Mountains treks. **Artuch (Artush) Alplager** at ~2200m is the standard starting point for the [[Kulikalon-Alaudin Loop]] — Pasrud village sits further down the same valley system, sometimes used as an exit or alternate trailhead.

`role:: trek trailhead`
`elevation_m:: 2200`
`days_here:: 1`

## Artuch Alplager (Mountain Camp)
The Soviet-era alplager (mountaineering basecamp) at 2200m, still functioning. Concrete buildings, basic facilities, used by trekkers, mountaineers, and as a road head for vehicles.

- Dormitory beds available (~150–200 TJS / ~$15–20)
- Tent pitches available for ~50 TJS
- Basic meals from the kitchen (plov, soup, tea)
- **No hot showers usually** — assume cold/none
- WiFi: don't count on it
- Walking distance from where the road ends

## Pasrud village
Lower in the valley (1700m), small farming village. Some trekkers use as exit point when descending via Mutnoe Lakes. Homestays available informally — ask via ZTDA.

## How to get there (Day 4)

### From [[Penjikent]] (~60 km)
- **Vehicle hire** required — no scheduled public transport
- 1.5–2 hours on degrading road (paved → broken paved → dirt)
- Cost: 250–350 TJS for a sedan (one-way), or share with other trekkers
- ZTDA or Hotel Umariyon arranges driver

### Vehicle quality
- Standard sedans manage to the alplager in dry conditions
- 4WD recommended if recent rain (October = shoulder season, can rain)
- If snow at altitude, may need to walk last 1–2 km

## Horseman + horse hire

> [!warning] Rates are seasonal and inflate fast — confirm with Caravanistan forum
> Rates rose ~50% over 2022–2024. Current (2024–2025) ballpark: **250–350 TJS / day** for horseman + one pack horse.

### What's standard
- Horseman walks alongside us, leading 1–2 horses
- Horses carry tents, sleeping bags, food, group gear — not riders
- Horseman knows route, sets camp, may help cook
- We carry day packs (water, snacks, layers, camera, valuables)
- Daily rate: [horseman_per_day_tjs:: 300] TJS (~$32 USD), 1 horse
- 5-day total for two: ~1500 TJS (~$160)
- Tip on completion: 200–500 TJS

### Booking the horseman
Three approaches:

1. **Walk-up at Artuch** — show up, ask kitchen staff or older men hanging around alplager. Old-school, works ~70% of the time in season. **Risky in October** as horsemen wind down.

2. **Pre-arrange via ZTDA** ([ztda-tourism.tj](https://ztda-tourism.tj/)) — Penjikent-based association, books horsemen, charges modest agency fee. Email weeks ahead.

3. **Pre-arrange via tour operator** — [Paramount Journey](https://paramountjourney.com/), [Hiking Tajikistan](https://hikingtajikistan.com/), [Trek Tajikistan](https://trektajikistan.com/), [Fann Journey](https://fannjourney.com/). More cost (typically +$50–80/day for a "guided" element on top of horseman), but locked-in and English-speaking.

> [!tip] October recommendation
> Pre-arrange via ZTDA or an operator. October the alplager is winding down — walk-up risk is real. Email/WhatsApp 4+ weeks before.

## Provisioning (already done in Penjikent)
- Carry: dry food (5 days for 2 + horseman), stove fuel, water (filter on trail), snacks, electrolytes
- Buy from [[Penjikent]] Panjshanbe Bazaar on Day 4 AM, ~800 TJS

## Day 4 plan
1. AM: provision food in Penjikent
2. Midday: drive Penjikent → Artuch (1.5–2h)
3. Afternoon: arrive Artuch, meet horseman, organize loads
4. **Acclimatization stroll** — 1–2 hours up valley toward Chukurak. **Don't gain more than 300m elevation.** Return for dinner.
5. Sleep alplager dorm or tent — first night at 2200m

## What's nearby (off-itinerary)
- [[Iskanderkul]] is in the same Fann complex but eastern side — separate trailhead system, not accessible by foot from Artuch in our timeframe
