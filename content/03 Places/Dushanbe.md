# Dushanbe

Capital of Tajikistan, ~800m. Wide Soviet boulevards, tree-lined parks, Persian-Soviet visual mashup. Our entry point (Day 1, arrival 01:00) and trek staging base (Day 2 prep).

`role:: arrival + trek staging`
`days_here:: 2`

## Things to see

### National Museum of Antiquities — **the headline**
Houses the **Sleeping Buddha** (Buddha in Nirvana, 14m long, 6th–7th century, excavated from Ajina-Tepa, the largest intact Buddha relic in Central Asia). Plus Sogdian frescoes (originals from Penjikent), Bronze Age artifacts from [[Penjikent|Sarazm]]. Worth 90 minutes.
- Address: Rajabovs 7, central Dushanbe
- Entry: ~25 TJS
- Closed Mondays

### Rudaki Park + central axis
Walk Rudaki Avenue from the Ismoili Somoni statue south past the parliament and library. Tree-lined, calm, good for jet-lag recovery. The 165m **flagpole** (formerly world's tallest until Jeddah took the title) is here.

### Mehrgon Bazaar
The market locals actually use, not the tourist one. Better than Shoh Mansur ("Green Bazaar") if you want range and atmosphere. Dried fruit, nuts, spices, plov ingredients. Open daily, busiest in morning.

### Hissar Fortress (optional half-day)
- 30km west, 30–40 min by taxi
- 17th-century madrassa, 3000-year-old fortifications, small museum
- Entry: [hissar_entry_tjs:: 10]
- Private taxi: [hissar_taxi_tjs:: 150] round trip, or marshrutka #8 to Zarnisor + shared taxi (5 TJS)
- Skip unless we have a buffer day; not in core 14-day plan

## Where to eat

### Tajik food (not Russian-Soviet holdovers)
- **Qurutob** — national dish, dairy-soaked flatbread + greens + yogurt sauce, eaten communally by hand. Best at older choyxonas. Ask locals "kurutob kuja" for current favorites.
- **Salom Namaste** (if it's still open) — Tajik food with Indian fusion, well-reviewed for non-Russian options
- **Merve** — Turkish-Tajik, reliable, central
- Plov is fine here but **better in Tashkent** — save the plov peak for there

### Bazaar lunches
Mehrgon Bazaar has stalls inside doing samsa, lagman, plov for ~30–60 TJS. Lower budget, more authentic, higher GI risk — judgment call.

## Where to sleep

> [!warning] Confirm late check-in for Day 1 arrival (01:00)
> Booking.com and direct-email guesthouses for confirmed 01:00 arrival. Some require deposit or full prepay for late arrivals.

Recent traveler favorites (verify current status 2 weeks before):
- **Green House Hostel** — long-running, Pamir/trek prep hub, dorm + private rooms, great info board. Older travelers note quality has varied; check recent reviews.
- **Yeti Hostel** — newer, similar trek-focused crowd
- **Mountain Apartment** rentals via Airbnb — best for two travelers wanting a kitchen and privacy
- **Atlas Hotel** or **Tajikistan Hotel** — mid-range, will absolutely confirm late check-in for a small fee

> [!tip] Twin vs. double room
> Most TJ guesthouses default to "double" (one bed). Email explicitly: "Twin beds requested — two separate beds, please." Russian: "Dva otdel'nykh krovat', pozhaluysta."

## Logistics

### From DYU airport (Day 1)
- **5km from center**, ~10 min by taxi
- Yandex Go works (after airport SIM/eSIM activated)
- Pre-arranged guesthouse pickup typically ~$10–20 — worth it at 01:00
- Cash: small TJS for arrival (have a $50 ready to break at airport exchange if needed)

### To Penjikent (Day 3)
- Shared taxis depart **Chorbog/Cement Zavod** area in northern Dushanbe (Varzob district)
- Same lot as Khujand taxis — search Yandex "Shared Taxi to Khujand"
- Cost: [tax_share_dyu_pjk_tjs:: 150] per seat (~$15), private hire ~$60–80
- Time: 5–6 hours over **Anzob Pass (3372m)** — paved road but slow, switchbacks
- Departures: early morning best (06:00–09:00), driver waits for 4-seat fill
- **Bring snacks and water.** No reliable rest stops on the pass.

### Trek prep (Day 2 priority)
- ATM for trek cash — see [[Money]]
- Buy stove fuel canisters (can't fly with them) — outdoor shop near Rudaki or ask at Green House
- Last-minute gear: microspikes, gaiters at outdoor shops if you didn't bring them. Limited selection — bring from home.
