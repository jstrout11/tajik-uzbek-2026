# Penjikent (Panjakent)

Provincial town in the Zerafshan Valley, our **trek staging point** (Day 3 in, Day 9 out) and the gateway between the Fann Mountains and Samarkand. ~30 km east of the Sarazm/Jartepa border.

`role:: trek staging + border gateway`
`days_here:: 1.5`

## Why we stop here
1. Provision food for the trek at Panjshanbe Bazaar
2. Pick up the horseman / finalize trek logistics with [[Artush and Pasrud]] contacts or ZTDA office
3. Cash buffer (last reliable ATM before Fann)
4. The Sogdian ruins are genuinely worth 2 hours if energy allows

## Things to see

### Ancient Penjikent (Sogdian ruins) — worth 2 hours
- **1.5 km SE of modern town**, on a terrace above the Zerafshan River
- The "Pompeii of Central Asia" — 5th–8th century Sogdian merchant city, abandoned after Arab conquest
- Excavated frescoes (hunting scenes, dancing girls) — **originals are split between the on-site museum, the National Museum in Dushanbe, and the Hermitage in St Petersburg.** The site has copies; combined with the on-site museum, gives a real sense of the city.
- Entry: ~20 TJS
- Best AM light; full visit ~2 hours including museum

### Sarazm UNESCO site (5500 years old)
- **15 km west** of Penjikent (on the way to the border)
- Bronze Age proto-urban settlement, "where the land begins"
- Famous for the "Princess of Sarazm" — woman buried ~3000 BC with lapis lazuli jewelry
- UNESCO listed since 2010
- Worth 30–45 min if combining with border crossing logistics (Day 9 has time pressure though)

### Rudaki statue + central park
The poet Rudaki was born nearby — the town has a statue and modest park. 15-min stop.

### Panjshanbe Bazaar — provisioning
The bazaar where we stock the trek pantry:
- Dry goods: oats, lentils, rice, pasta, dried fruit, nuts
- Fresh: hard cheese (chechil), non bread, sausage if you eat it
- Sweets: halva, dried apricots (the famous local ones)
- Eggs: pack in egg-carton, horseman wraps in cloth
- Allow 1 hour, bring big bags

`provision_budget_for_two_for_five_days_tjs:: 800`

## Where to sleep

### Hotel Umariyon — recommended
- **Address:** Prospekt Rudaki, 157, Panjakent
- 3-floor hotel, 15 rooms, central location
- Twin rooms available, A/C, WiFi, breakfast included
- **Cash only** — bring TJS
- Rating: 8.6 on Booking.com
- Cost: [umariyon_twin_usd:: 35] approx (verify at booking)
- [Booking.com link](https://www.booking.com/hotel/tj/umarion.html)

### Homestays
- ZTDA ([Zarafshan Tourism Development Association](https://ztda-tourism.tj/)) arranges homestays in town and across the valley
- Cheaper than Hotel Umariyon, more cultural, twin-bed availability inconsistent
- ~80–150 TJS per person per night with breakfast

> [!tip] Day 9 stay
> Day 9 is the trek-out day with border crossing. Most plans push straight to Samarkand same day (long but doable). If exhausted, sleep one night in Penjikent and cross border AM Day 10 — costs us one Samarkand evening.

## Trek logistics — what we do here

### Day 3 (arrive late afternoon)
- Check into Hotel Umariyon, drop bags
- Wander old town + Rudaki Park
- Dinner at hotel or small choyxona
- Confirm horseman arrangement for Day 4 (phone or via guesthouse)
- Sogdian ruins if energy

### Day 4 (departure to trailhead)
- Breakfast at hotel
- **Panjshanbe Bazaar provisioning** — 90 min, 800 TJS for two
- Cash for horseman fee in TJS small bills
- Driver pickup to Artuch — pre-arranged via [[ZTDA]] or local contact, 1.5–2h to Artuch (~$30–50 per vehicle)

### Day 9 (trek out, transit)
- Trek exit → vehicle to Penjikent (lunch break) → border → Samarkand
- Tight day; carry extra snacks, expect a full 10–12h door-to-door

## Logistics

### From Dushanbe (Day 3)
- Shared taxi 5–6h via Anzob Pass — see [[Dushanbe]]
- Cost: 150 TJS ($15) shared, $60–80 private hire

### To Samarkand (Day 9)
- Shared taxi Penjikent → Sarazm/Jartepa border ~45 min
- Walk crossing
- Shared taxi Jartepa → Samarkand ~45 min
- Total cost: ~$20 per person, ~2.5–3h door-to-door (excluding waiting)

### To trailhead (Day 4)
- Penjikent → Artuch Alplager: 1.5–2h on rough road (paved → dirt)
- Vehicle hire: ~250–350 TJS one-way for sedan or share with other trekkers
- ZTDA office in Penjikent arranges; phone numbers via [ztda-tourism.tj](https://ztda-tourism.tj/)

## Where to eat
Small selection — choyxonas around the bazaar do lagman, plov, shashlik. Hotel Umariyon café is reliable for breakfast + dinner. **Not a food destination** — Penjikent is a working town.
