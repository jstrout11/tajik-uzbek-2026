> [!warning] Get 2–3 quotes
> Email at least 3 operators with the same brief (5-day Kulikalon-Alaudin, 2 trekkers, mid-October, horseman + transport, no guide). Compare prices and responsiveness. Reply quality is itself the signal.

## Booking
- **railway.uz** — official Uzbekistan railways, book Afrosiyob 1–2 weeks ahead. English version works. App: "UZ Railways" or "Tasit"
- **Booking.com** — solid in UZ, hit-or-miss in TJ (Hotel Umariyon is on it though)
- **Direct WhatsApp/email** to guesthouses — often the only reliable way in TJ, and gets you better rates

## Maps + navigation

### Offline first
- **[Maps.me](https://maps.me/)** or **[Organic Maps](https://organicmaps.app/)** — OSM offline. **Download TJ + UZ packs in Denver before flight.** Critical for Fann (Google Maps is useless there).
- **[Gaia GPS](https://www.gaiagps.com/)** — topo + trails for the trek, paid subscription. Pre-download regional tiles.
- **[Caltopo](https://caltopo.com/)** — desktop, export GPX for Gaia

### For the trek specifically
- Search OSM / Gaia for "Kulikalon Alauddin Loop" — community-tracked GPX exists
- Import GPX to Garmin inReach Mini for backup navigation

## Guidebooks
- **Bradt Tajikistan**, Sophie Ibbotson — 3rd edition, 2021. The most current and detailed dedicated TJ guide. **Buy it.**
- **Lonely Planet Central Asia** — last major edition 2018, **dated**. Still useful for general context and city walks, but **don't trust prices or open/closed status**.
- **The Lost Heart of Asia**, Colin Thubron — travelogue, beautifully written, sets the mood
- **The Great Game**, Peter Hopkirk — background on Russian/British imperial rivalry in Central Asia
- **Shadow of the Silk Road**, Thubron — another Thubron, also worth it

## Weather + conditions
- **[Mountain-Forecast Fann](https://www.mountain-forecast.com/peaks/Chimtarga)** — peak-level forecasts, multiple altitudes per peak
- **[Windy.com](https://www.windy.com/)** — best general weather visualization, ECMWF model
- **NOAA + UK Met Office** — long-range pressure pattern check for the trek window
- Check forecasts daily Days 1–4 in Dushanbe/Penjikent; the call to commit to Kulikalon-Alaudin vs. pivot to [[Seven Lakes (Haft Kul)|Haft Kul]] is made Day 3 evening

## Embassy + emergency
- **US Embassy Dushanbe** — Somoni Avenue, +992 37 229 2300, [tj.usembassy.gov](https://tj.usembassy.gov/)
- **US Embassy Tashkent** — 3 Moyqorghon St, Block 5, +998 78 120 5450, [uz.usembassy.gov](https://uz.usembassy.gov/)
- Save both phone numbers offline on phone
- **Garmin inReach SOS** is the right path for remote-mountain emergency — embassy is for passport/legal/medical-evac coordination
