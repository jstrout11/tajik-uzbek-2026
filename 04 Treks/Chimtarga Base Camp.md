# Chimtarga Base Camp

The deeper Fann route — multi-day to the base of **Chimtarga Peak (5489m)**, the highest in the range, via Mutnye Lakes and the Chimtarga Pass at 4750m. **Not viable for our October trip** — saved here for a future July–September attempt.

`days:: 7`
`max_elevation_m:: 4750`
`difficulty:: very_hard`
`october_viability:: snowed_out`
`recommended:: false (parked)`

## Why not this trip

> [!warning] October snowed out
> Chimtarga Pass at 4750m is well above the October snow line. By early October even in dry years the pass holds snow; by mid-October it's a winter-conditions traverse requiring mountaineering kit (ice axe, crampons, real avalanche awareness). Outside our trip's risk tolerance and gear.

The seven-day commitment also eats more days than our 14-day skeleton can absorb without dropping a UZ city.

## What it would look like (future trip)

### Outline route
1. Artuch → Kulikalon Lakes
2. Kulikalon → Alaudin Pass → Alaudin Lakes
3. Alaudin Lakes → Mutnye Lakes (3500m)
4. Acclimatization day at Mutnye
5. Mutnye → **Chimtarga Pass (4750m)** → upper Iskanderdarya valley
6. Descent toward Iskanderkul side
7. Out to [[Iskanderkul]] or Sarytag village

### Season
- **July–early September only**
- Even in season, expect snow on Chimtarga Pass
- Carry ice axe, basic crampons or aluminum mountaineering boots, rope for the partner

### Operators
- Full guided trip recommended for Chimtarga — too much pass exposure for independent / horseman-only
- **Paramount Journey** and **Trek Tajikistan** offer 7–9 day Chimtarga itineraries

## Why park rather than delete
- Mountaineer is on the Ama Dablam pipeline track (per related project [[Turkish Airlines flight search project]])
- Chimtarga + Lenin Peak (Pamir, Kyrgyz side) is a logical 2027 or 2028 trip — high-altitude conditioning for bigger objectives
- This file is the seed for that planning
